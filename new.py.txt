Article 21 of the Indian Constitution guarantees that "No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law". Available to citizens and non-citizens, this fundamental right has been broadly interpreted by the Supreme Court to encompass the right to live with human dignity, privacy, health, and a clean environment.

Key Aspects of Article 21
Fundamental Right: It is a cornerstone of the Indian Constitution, often called the heart of fundamental rights, and cannot be suspended even during emergencies.
Protection Against State Action: It protects against arbitrary state action, ensuring that any deprivation of life or liberty must be through a fair, just, and reasonable legal procedure.
Expanded Scope: The Supreme Court has expanded the definition of "life" beyond mere animal existence, incorporating numerous sub-rights.

Judicially Recognized Rights under Article 21
Through various judgments, the Supreme Court has included the following within the scope of Article 21: 
Right to Privacy: Recognized as a fundamental right.
Right to Dignity: The right to live with human dignity.
Right to Health & Medical Care: Includes the right to mental and physical well-being.
Right to Livelihood: Part of the right to life.
Right to Shelter: Protection of a place to live.
Right to Environment: Right to pollution-free water and air.
Right to Education: Reinforced by Article 21A.
Right to Travel Abroad: As held in the Maneka Gandhi case.
Right to Free Legal Aid & Speedy Trial: For fair justice.
